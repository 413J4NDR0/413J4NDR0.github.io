Summer of Cinema 2019

Films

Pokémon Detective Pikachu (2019)

As a fan of Pokémon since Ruby and Sapphire, I was reluctantly excited for this movie. Hollywood has not been friendly to videogame adaptations. But brilliant world building and character design brought justice to this beloved franchise, despite a dumb plot and laboured storytelling.
Watched at my local theatre Century at Tanforan on 7 June 2019

John Wick: Chapter 3 - Parabellum (2019)

I watched the John Wick film series in three days, culminating with the third installment at Tanforan. Though some of the symbolism is lost in the fray, tight production delivers unrelenting, beautiful action every time. The John Wick films have marked and executed my heart.
Watched at my local theatre Century at Tanforan on 18 June 2019

Toy Story 4 (2019)

I was wary of the new Toy Story film when it was first announced. I thought the third film was a perfect conclusion to the series. Nevertheless, I went to the opening fan event and was met with a solid film that has lost some of the magic of the earlier installments.
Watched at my local theatre Century at Tanforan on 20 June 2019

The Dead Don't Die (2019)

This film can be summed up by Officer Ronnie Peterson's refrain throughout the film: "This isn't going to end well". Well Ronnie, you were right about that. The plot ambles aimlessly like the undead, while the cynical production and metareferential jokes fall flat.
Watched at my local theatre Century at Tanforan on 25 June 2019
