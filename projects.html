Classical Codes and Unitary Designs for Quantum Computing

This is a research independent study I am undertaking during the Fall 2018 semester supervised by Dr. Robert Calderbank. The focus will be on the Heisenberg-Weyl group \(HW_N\), where \(N = 2^m\); the family of Heisenberg groups are closely tied to the foundation of quantum mechanics. The Heisenberg-Weyl group consists of the Pauli matrices of size \(N\), and this independent study has the following objectives:

  1. The Heisenberg-Weyl and Clifford Groups: Let \(S = \{ I_N, -I_N, i I_N , -iI_N\} \), which is a subgroup of \(HW_N\). Prove that the graph \( \Gamma_N \) constructed from the quotient group \(HW_N/S\) is strongly regular and that the automorphism group \(\text{aut}(\Gamma_N)\) is the Clifford Group \(\text{Cliff}_N\)
  2. Pauli Mixing: Translate the concepts of Pauli Mixing to the transitivity of group actions by \(\text{Cliff}_N\) on the vertices, edges, and non-edges of \(\Gamma_N\). Prove that the Clifford ensemble \(G\) is Pauli Mixing
  3. Unitary 2- and 3-Designs: Prove that Pauli Mixing implies that the Clifford ensemble is a unitary 2-design and that Pauli 2-Mixing implies that the Clifford ensemble is a 3-design
